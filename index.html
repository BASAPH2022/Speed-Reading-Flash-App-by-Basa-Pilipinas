üî¨ The Science Behind Speed Reading

Research from cognitive psychology shows that the average person reads at 200-300 words per minute, but the human brain can process visual information much faster. Speed reading techniques work by:


1. Eliminating Subvocalization: Most people "hear" words in their head while reading, limiting speed to speaking pace (150-250 WPM). Our flash training breaks this habit.


2. Expanding Visual Span: Instead of reading word-by-word, trained readers can process 3-5 words per eye fixation, dramatically reducing reading time.


3. Reducing Regression: Untrained readers often re-read text unconsciously. Our progressive training builds confidence and reduces backward eye movements.


4. Improving Comprehension: Contrary to popular belief, faster reading often leads to better comprehension as the brain stays more engaged with the material.
